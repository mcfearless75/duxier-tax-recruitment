THE PROBLEM

The fee stopped buying anything most firms actually need.

Traditional tax recruiters charge 20–30% of first-year salary per hire — often £15,000+ for one appointment. That fee used to buy sourcing, screening and discretion. Increasingly, it just buys access to a database the candidate would hand over directly, for free, if they had a safe way to do it.

CONFIDENTIALITY, BY DESIGN

Anonymisation isn't a checkbox here. It's the product.

A name blacked out on a PDF isn't confidential if the qualification, dates and location still point straight back to one person. We generalise every field that could identify you before it's ever searchable.

01

Employer generalised

Firm names replaced with sector and size band — "Top 20 practice," not the practice itself.

02

Dates ranged

Exact start and end dates converted to PQE ranges, never precise enough to trace.

03

Location broadened

Town and postcode become region — specific enough to be useful, not specific enough to identify.

04

Identity locked

Name and contact details never appear until you personally approve a reveal request.
